How It Works

A simple path for sunlight to enter buildings

1. Sunlight collection

Sunlight is collected on the rooftop using optical components designed to capture usable visible light while avoiding excessive heat.

The system works best during daytime hours when natural light is abundantly available.

2. Light transmission

The collected sunlight is guided through high-efficiency optical fiber. Unlike electrical cables, the fiber carries light directly—without converting it into electricity.

This allows daylight to travel from the roof to interior spaces, even across multiple floors.

3. Indoor distribution

At the indoor end, luminaires and diffusers distribute the sunlight evenly, creating a soft and natural lighting environment.

The goal is not brightness alone, but visual comfort and consistency.

4. Hybrid lighting approach

Artificial lighting is integrated into the system to support indoor illumination during evenings, nights, and low-sun conditions.

This ensures uninterrupted lighting while reducing daytime energy consumption.

Why this approach matters

  • Reduces daytime electricity usage
  • Brings real daylight into deep-plan buildings
  • Improves visual comfort and indoor experience
  • Complements existing lighting infrastructure
